# 🎯 MA Tech Lead Landing Playbook
## Simple, Proven Way to Get Your First Clients

---

## Phase 1: Find Your Ideal Customers (Week 1)

### Target Companies (Pick ONE Industry Focus First)
**Best to start with:**
- E-commerce businesses (struggling with outdated sites)
- SaaS startups (need feature development)
- Local service businesses (need web/booking systems)
- Digital marketing agencies (need custom tools)
- Small-medium enterprises (10-100 employees)

### Where to Find Them
1. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** - Filter by: Industry + Company Size + Location
2. **Google Maps** - Search your target industry in your area + nearby cities
3. **Industry directories** - Ask Google: "[industry] directory [your region]"
4. **Facebook Groups** - Join local business groups, look for decision makers
5. **Referrals** - Ask friends/family if they know anyone

### Research Before Reaching Out (5 min per person)
- Visit their website (What's outdated? What's working?)
- Check LinkedIn (Who's the right person to contact?)
- Look at their social media (Do they post? Engaged audience?)
- Note 1-2 specific things you observe

**Write it down:** [Company] | [Decision Maker] | [Main Problem They Have]

## Phase 2: The Outreach (Week 2-3)

### Step 1: First Email (Day 1)
**Use:** `lead-cold-outreach-v2.html`

**Formula:**
1. Genuine compliment about their business
2. Identify ONE specific problem you noticed
3. Brief mention of similar company you helped
4. Ask for 15 min conversation
5. Make it easy to say yes (calendar link)

**Send to:** Decision maker directly (CEO, Founder, Owner, Marketing Manager)
**Best Time:** Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-2pm
**Number to Send:** 5-10 per week (not all at once)

---

### Step 2: Follow-up Email (Day 7 if no response)
**Use:** `lead-follow-up-v2.html`

**Key Points:**
- Don't apologize for following up
- Share specific result from similar client
- Restate the value
- Make it even easier to say yes

---

### Step 3: Final Follow-up (Day 14 if still no response)
**Message:** "One last thing"

Keep it SHORT:

> Hi [Name],
>
> I get it—busy inbox, lots going on. One last time: if you ever want to explore how [Company Name] could [solve their problem], I'm here. Otherwise, best of luck with everything.
>
> Talk soon (maybe),
> [Your Name]

Then move on to next prospect.

---

## Phase 3: When They Respond (The Conversation)

### They Respond to Email ✓
Reply same day, within 2 hours if possible.

**Script:**
> Thanks for getting back to me, [Name]. I appreciate it.
>
> Real quick—the reason I reached out is [their specific problem]. I've seen this pattern with [similar company], and we helped them [specific result].
>
> But I want to hear from you first. When you think about [their business], what's the biggest friction point right now? [one specific question]

### They Book a Call ✓
**Before the call:**
- Do more research on their company
- Prepare 2-3 specific questions
- Have a notepad ready
- Test your audio/video

**During the call (15 min):**
1. **Ask questions (70% of time)**
   - What are they building?
   - What's working? What's not?
   - What keeps them up at night?
   - Why does this matter to them?

2. **Listen (don't pitch)**
   - Take notes
   - Let them talk
   - Ask follow-ups

3. **Explore fit (last 3 min)**
   - "Does it make sense to explore this further?"
   - If yes: "Let me put something simple together and we'll review it together"
   - If no: "Appreciate your time. If anything changes, reach out."

4. **Next step is clear**
   - Always end with: "Here's what happens next..."

## Phase 4: Turn Interest Into Commitment

### After the Call
- **If positive:** Send them a simple 1-page summary of what you discussed + next steps
- **If lukewarm:** Give them one piece of free insight/advice via email
- **If not interested:** Thank them, stay in touch, move on

### The Proposal
Don't jump to proposal too fast.

**Right approach:**
1. Call #1 = Discovery (you listen)
2. Email = Summary + Your Recommendation
3. Call #2 = Walk through what you suggest + timeline + investment
4. Proposal = Final details + contract

This is NOT a pushy sales process. It's a conversation about solving their problem.

---

## Phase 5: Closing

### The Closing Call
**What you say:**
> "Based on everything we discussed, here's what I recommend: [solution]. This would take [timeline], cost [investment], and result in [specific outcome]. Does this feel like the right move for you?"

### If they say YES
- Get commitment (name + date)
- Send contract same day
- Start immediately

### If they say "Let me think about it"
- "What would help you feel more confident?"
- Listen to real objection
- Address it
- "So if [concern] wasn't an issue, would you move forward?"

### If they say NO
- "What would need to be different for this to work?"
- Listen
- Either adjust proposal OR
- "No pressure. If anything changes, let's reconnect"
- (Stay in touch, follow up in 6 months)

## Your First Month: Simple Numbers

**Goal: Land 1 Client**

### Week 1: Research & Outreach Prep
- Identify 15 ideal companies
- Research decision makers
- Prepare first email

### Week 2-3: Send Emails & Get Meetings
- Send 10-15 first emails
- Expect 2-3 responses
- Get 1-2 calls scheduled

### Week 4: Have Conversations & Close
- Take 1-2 discovery calls
- Send proposal to interested prospect
- Close 1 deal

**Math:**
- 15 outreach = 2-3 responses (10-20% response rate)
- 2-3 responses = 1-2 first calls
- 1-2 first calls = 1 proposal
- 1 proposal = typically 50-70% close rate

**So: 15 outreach → 1 client**

## The Tone You Must Have

**DO:**
✅ Be genuinely interested in THEIR problem
✅ Listen more than you talk
✅ Be honest about fit
✅ Keep it casual and human
✅ Show you did research
✅ Make it easy for them

**DON'T:**
❌ Sound like a salesperson
❌ Pitch too early
❌ Be pushy or desperate
❌ Use corporate jargon
❌ Follow up more than 3 times
❌ Send generic mass emails

## Common Objections & Responses

### "We're not looking for new services right now"
**You:** "Totally get it. But can I ask—when you DO have a project, how do you typically find the right team?"

### "We already have a developer"
**You:** "Cool, that's smart. Question: Are they currently heads-down on everything, or do they have capacity for new projects?"

### "This seems expensive"
**You:** "Fair question. What's your current cost of [their pain point]? Because usually the cost of NOT solving it is higher."

### "Can you send me a proposal?"
**You:** "I could, but honestly? I'd rather have a 15-min call first so I make sure the proposal actually solves YOUR problem, not just a generic one. Sound good?"
